Founding Women

 

 

Judy Donner is Coordinator of Development and Alumni Relations at the School of International Service at American University.

Lisa Herrick, Ph.D. is a Clinical Psychologist who is in private practice in Washington D.C. and Northern Virginia. She works with children, couples, and families, as well as divorced parents in Parent Coordination.

Naomi Karp is a public interest attorney who works for AARP's Public Policy Institute. She has dedicated her career to enhancing the legal rights of low-income and older people, and currently focuses on elder abuse, guardianship, and health care decision-making.

Amy Kossoff, MD is caring for homeless, indigent patients in Washington, DC.

Caroline Newman was a co-founder of WW and brought her enthusiastic grin and excitement to everything we did. She died suddenly in December 2008 and is missed greatly. She was executive editor at Smithsonian Books, where she acquired trade, non-fiction, and illustrated books.

Anne Wallace is the Executive Director of the Osher Lifelong Learning Institute at American University, an academic program for those whose curiosity never retires.
